Find us. Everyone is welcome.

We gather in the street, there are some announcements, then we ride together at a party pace. Bring lights, water, and whatever wheels help you move comfortably. The ride is typically 14 miles, 8–12mph, and always ends at the meeting spot.

Why we ride

Streets should move people.

Safe streets are a human right

Everyone deserves dignity, comfort, and safety, even and especially when they are outside of a car.

People over machines

We challenge car-centered design and planning, and we demand better from our leaders.

Accountable city leadership

We want real traffic calming, protected bike lanes, actual bus rapid transit, and safe streets that protect people.

All on the same team

This is not bike supremacy. Pedestrians, mobility-device users, bicycles, e-bikes, skaters, scooter riders, transit riders, and more all deserve safe, comfortable streets with dignity.

Leaderless

Critical Mass has no governing body, and Denver Critical Mass is not a chapter of anything. Organizers and stewards are regular people helping the ride move together.

Anyone can help, and anyone can lead a ride.

Welcoming Protest

Even though this is a protest, we are not here to be aggressive. The point is not to replace car dominance with bike dominance.

We aim to be a welcoming ride for all people, on and off the ride, and a friendly, fun spectacle for our neighbors.

Shared Care

Respect pedestrians' space and comfort, especially around crosswalks. Respect buses and transit riders too; public transit and pedestrian dignity are part of the future we ride for.

Worldwide

No headquarters. No franchise. Everywhere.

Critical Mass has rolled across every inhabited continent. Each city makes it its own, but the pattern is familiar: people gather, ride together, and demand safe streets for everyone.
